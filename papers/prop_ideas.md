# PROP Algebras and the Weaves Framework

## Background: PROs, PROPs, and Their Algebras

A **PRO** is a strict monoidal category whose monoid of objects is freely generated by a
single element x — equivalently, every object is a tensor power x^⊗n for some n ∈ ℕ.
Morphisms x^⊗m → x^⊗n are abstract operations with m inputs and n outputs. There is no
symmetry requirement — wire order matters. (The common presentation "objects are the
natural numbers, monoidal product is addition" is the canonical skeletal representative of
this; any strict monoidal category with a single generating object is a PRO up to
equivalence.)

A **PROP** (PROducts and Permutations) is a PRO equipped with symmetry morphisms
σ_{m,n}: x^⊗(m+n) → x^⊗(n+m) satisfying the symmetric monoidal axioms. Wires can now be
freely crossed. A **colored PROP** generalizes this by replacing the single generator x
with a set of colors O; objects are then finite lists over O (the free monoid O*), and
morphisms are typed operations mixing multiple wire types.

An **algebra** of a PROP P in a symmetric monoidal category C is a strong symmetric
monoidal functor F: P → C. It assigns to the generator x some object F(x) ∈ C and to
each morphism θ: m → n a concrete operation F(θ): F(x)^⊗m → F(x)^⊗n, respecting
composition and the monoidal structure. A morphism of algebras F → G is a monoidal
natural transformation — a family of maps φ_X: F(X) → G(X) commuting with every
operation. The **endomorphism PROP** End(X) of an object X ∈ C has hom-sets
End(X)(m,n) = C(X^⊗m, X^⊗n); an algebra of P in X is equivalently a PROP morphism
P → End(X).

In the pyncd setting, `BroadcastedCategory` is the colored PROP and
`ConstructedModule.construct()` is the algebra functor into PyTorch — see *The Direct
Identification* below for the precise correspondence. The rest of this document develops
the consequences of making that identification explicit.

Classical examples from mathematics: algebras of the simplex PROP Δ₊ are monoids;
algebras of its opposite are comonoids; algebras of Mat(k) (matrices over a field) are
bicommutative bialgebras; algebras of the cobordism PROP 2Cob are Frobenius algebras
(underpinning 2D TQFTs). PROPs also admit **presentations by generators and relations**,
analogous to group presentations — this is the basis for graphical calculi such as the
ZX-calculus and Interacting Hopf Algebras.

## The Direct Identification

The compilation pipeline *is* already a PROP algebra, just unnamed as such.

`BroadcastedCategory` is a **colored PROP**: objects are finite lists of typed arrays
(`Array[B, A]`), the monoidal product is wire juxtaposition (`ProductOfMorphisms`),
symmetries are `Rearrangement` morphisms, and colors are `(Datatype, shape)` pairs.
Morphisms are `Broadcasted` objects — abstract, shape-typed operations with no concrete
numerical content.

`ConstructedModule.construct()` is the **algebra functor** F: Br → PyTorch:
- On objects: `Array[Reals, (d₁, d₂)]` → `torch.Tensor` of shape `(d₁, d₂)`
- On morphisms: `Broadcasted` → an `nn.Module` implementing the broadcasting contraction

A *randomly initialized* model is one algebra; a *trained* model is a different point in
the space of algebra functors with the same abstract source morphism. The architecture
**is** the PROP expression; the weights parameterize the algebra.

## The Para Refinement

The learned layers (Linear, Embedding) make the algebra **parameterized** — the functor
carries a weight space. This is exactly the **Para construction** (Gavranovic et al.,
arXiv:2402.15332): Para(C) is a bicategory where a 1-cell A → B is a pair (P, f: P ⊗ A → B)
with P an explicit parameter object. A trained model is a specific section of this
parameterized algebra over the weight space. Gradient descent is a path in that section space.

The pyncd setup implicitly uses this: `Multilinear` bundles the weight tensor P and the
forward function f: P × A → B. Making this explicit as a Para morphism would give a clean
categorical account of what training *does* to the algebra.

## Algebra Morphisms = Weight Tying and Equivariance

A morphism of PROP algebras F → G is a monoidal natural transformation — a family of
morphisms φ_X: F(X) → G(X) commuting with every operation. In the deep learning context:

- **Weight tying**: two components of the model share one weight tensor. Formally, the
  shared-weight model is related to the unshared model by a natural transformation that
  collapses two distinct parameters to a diagonal. This is a structural statement about
  the algebra, not an engineering convention.
- **Equivariance**: if a group G acts on the axis structure (e.g., permutations of token
  positions), then an equivariant architecture is an algebra that factors through the
  quotient PROP — the fixed points under the G-action. An equivariant layer is one where
  φ_g ∘ F(θ) = F(θ) ∘ φ_g for all PROP morphisms θ and group elements g. Proving this
  becomes a statement in the PROP algebra, derivable from the generators-and-relations
  presentation.

## Creative Opportunities

### 1. Generators-and-Relations Presentations of Architectures

A transformer can be presented as a colored PROP with generators {embed, Q-proj, K-proj,
V-proj, softmax-attn, FFN, layernorm, residual} and relations encoding the transformer
equations. An algebra of this PROP in FVect *is* a concrete transformer. Properties like
"attention is equivariant under token permutation" become theorems about the PROP
presentation, derivable from the generators — not experiments.

This would give pyncd a formal "architecture grammar": composable, type-checked, and
amenable to algebraic proof.

### 2. Diagrammatic Rewriting for Compilation

PROP presentations make equational reasoning graphical: rewrite rules are PROP equations.
The PyTorch compilation pipeline already does this informally (operator fusion, stride
absorption). A formal presentation would make graph rewrites **provably sound** —
analogous to how the ZX-calculus gives a complete rewriting system for quantum circuits.
The `Composed` / `ProductOfMorphisms` normal form is already a step in this direction.

### 3. The Bool Semiring and Interacting Hopf Algebras

The codebase already implements a Bool semiring (∃ as addition, ∧ as multiplication). The
**Interacting Hopf Algebras** PROP (IH_R) over the Boolean semiring has a completeness
theorem: every true equation in Boolean linear algebra is derivable from two graphically
presented Hopf structures. This is directly relevant to the Bool semiring morphisms in
`BroadcastedCategory` — the graphical calculus for masked attention, reachability, and
transitive closure might be an instance of IH_Bool. Worth investigating whether the
existing Bool semiring operations close under IH axioms.

### 4. Free Algebras and Architecture Initialization

The **free algebra** on a PROP P (via the Yoneda embedding P → Set^{P^op}) corresponds
to the "no-parameter" model: the architecture with no learned weights, only structural
operations. Every concrete model (weighted functor) factors uniquely through the free
algebra via the universal property. In practice: any initialization scheme for a given
architecture is a morphism from the free algebra. The universality of the free algebra
encodes the fact that the *architecture alone* (without weights) already determines which
operations are possible — and that any two weight initializations are related by a
morphism of parameterized algebras.

### 5. Model Compression as Approximate Algebra Morphisms

Knowledge distillation — compressing a large model into a smaller one — is the problem of
finding an algebra morphism φ: F_large → F_small that approximately commutes with all
operations. The PROP structure makes "same architecture, different capacity" precise: it
distinguishes morphisms between algebras of the *same* PROP expression (same architecture)
from morphisms between algebras of *different* PROP expressions (different architectures).
Current distillation literature does not use this distinction; the PROP framing suggests a
typeful hierarchy of compression strategies.

### 6. The StrideCategory as a Representation-Theoretic Object

`StrideCategory` is itself a PROP (or at least a PRO). Its algebras in FVect are "index
representations": functors assigning a vector space to each axis-list and a linear map to
each stride matrix. These are a kind of **representation theory of the index algebra** —
and the constraint that a model be equivariant under an axis permutation is the condition
that the StrideCategory algebra morphism commutes with the permutation. This reframes
geometric deep learning (equivariant networks) as representation theory of the
StrideCategory.

---

## Summary

The most immediate and concrete connection: **`construct()` is an algebra functor, and
naming it as such unlocks the full toolkit** — algebra morphisms for weight tying and
equivariance, generators-and-relations for architecture specification, diagrammatic
rewriting for provably-sound compiler optimization, and the Para construction for a clean
account of training. The Bool semiring / IH connection is the most mathematically
surprising candidate for new theory.
